About Us

The Caribbean Centre for Educational Planning (CCEP) is an entity of the University of the West Indies and was established in October 2016.

The Centre provides technical support to countries and educational institutions in the Caribbean and Latin America, in the areas of strategic planning and policy advising, as well as undertakes research in support of its strategic planning and policy advice undertakings.


The Centre’s activities have included:

Research on

  • Tertiary education faculty attitudes to, and perspectives on, involvement in strategic planning;
  • School administrators’ and other stakeholders’ attitudes towards school improving planning;
  • The nexus between education and development
  • Philosophical assumptions that inform educational policy in Jamaica

Symposia and Thought Leadership Forums on:

  • A Conversation with the National Education Inspectorate (Forum)
  • Planning in higher education: Whither prospects for economic growth and development (Forum)
  • Sustainable Educational Planning: Progress And Prospects In The Caribbean Region (Symposium)
  • Learning from the COVID-19 Pandemic: Imperatives and Opportunities for Building Resilient Education Systems in the Caribbean (Webinar)
  • Reimagining Education: Pre and Post Covid 19 Considerations (Webinar)
  • Strengthening Infrastructure Support and Creativity in Delivery: Ensuring Every Child has Access to Quality Education. (Webinar)
  • Training organizational leaders in strategic planning (across the Caribbean)
  • Providing consulting services to schools and other organizations in developing their strategic plans.

RATIONALE FOR THE CCEP

  • To be a resource for housing and dissemination of data on, and to educational planning in the region
  • To undertake capacity-building in educational planning with key personnel in the Ministries of Education in the Region
  • To provide technical assistance in the development, implementation, monitoring and evaluation of educational plans
  • To advise on the development and implementation of critical educational policy to meet development needs in the education systems across the Caribbean region.

HISTORY AND MANDATE OF CCEP

  • Established in September 2016, began formal operations in March of 2017
  • Launched in Barbados in June of 2017 and in Jamaica in March, 2018
  • Execution of competency-based training in strategic educational planning
  • Development and evaluation of strategic educational plans for educational institutions and countries;
  • Undertaking research to inform new approaches to educational planning and to inform educational policy

VISION STATEMENT OF THE CCEP

An organization which enables countries in the Caribbean and Latin America to acquire sustainable capacities in educational planning, resulting in a higher quality of life for citizens.


MISSION STATEMENT OF THE CCEP

To support regional development and improved productivity through the provision of high quality, relevant, educational planning services to countries of the Caribbean and Latin America.


CORE VALUES

  • Collaboration: pursuing alliances and strategic relationships in seeking to advance the mission of the organization
  • Equity: ensuring that the resources being deployed by the CCEP focus on institutions and countries that are most needy
  • Inclusivity: facilitating access and participation regardless of social class, ethnicity or geographical location
  • Sustainability: designing interventions in ways that assure capacity-building and long-term value creation

THE CCEP’S VALUE PROPOSITION

Using an inclusive and contextually relevant approach, the CCEP provides educational planning services to countries and educational institutions in the Caribbean and Latin America, designed to build organizational capacities and improve organizational performance and outcomes.

THE CCEP’S CORE BUSINESS

  • Competency-based training in strategic educational planning;
  • Developing and evaluating strategic educational plans for educational institutions and countries;
  • Research designed to inform new approaches to educational planning and to inform educational policy.

FEATURES OF BUSINESS MODEL

  • Facilitating consultations and training events for highlighting, enriching, and promoting effective and sustainable practice models in educational planning;
  • Franchising courses on behalf of world class entities involved in technical training of educational planning practitioners and offering such courses to regional practitioners, educational institutions and ministries of education;
  • Formulating customized and contextually relevant products and services in educational capacity-building and institutional strengthening in response to customer demand;
  • Engaging the services of educational planning professionals to across the Region so as to be able to respond to multiple service demands concurrently;
  • Regional reach in providing support at all levels of the education system;
  • Pursuing research to inform educational policy, practice, and planning.
