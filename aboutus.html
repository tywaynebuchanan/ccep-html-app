About Us

The Caribbean Centre for Educational Planning (CCEP) is an entity of the University of the West Indies and was established in October 2016. The Centre provides technical support to countries and educational institutions in the Caribbean and Latin America, in the areas of strategic planning and policy advising, as well as undertakes research in support of its strategic planning and policy advice undertakings. The Centre’s activities have included: Research on: Tertiary education faculty attitudes to, and perspectives on, involvement in strategic planning; School administrators’ and other stakeholders’ attitudes towards school improving planning; The nexus between education and development Philosophical assumptions that inform educational policy in Jamaica Symposia and Thought Leadership Forums on: A Conversation with the National Education Inspectorate (Forum) Planning in higher education: Whither prospects for economic growth and development (Forum) Sustainable Educational Planning: Progress And Prospects In The Caribbean Region (Symposium) Learning from the COVID-19 Pandemic: Imperatives and Opportunities for Building Resilient Education Systems in the Caribbean (Webinar) Reimagining Education: Pre and Post Covid 19 Considerations (Webinar) Strengthening Infrastructure Support and Creativity in Delivery: Ensuring Every Child has Access to Quality Education. (Webinar) Training organizational leaders in strategic planning (across the Caribbean) Providing consulting services to schools and other organizations in developing their strategic plans.
